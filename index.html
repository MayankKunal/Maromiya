Residential Institutions
Parivaar runs 6 residential institutions (2 in Bengal & 4 in Madhya Pradesh) with more than 3300 children from the most destitute, vulnerable and poor tribal backgrounds. There are more than 2100 children in our Bengal residentials (more than 1000 in boys and girls campus each) and 1250 plus children in Madhya Pradesh residentials.
SEVA KUTIRS

India's Largest Free Program on Education and Nutrition for Children from Tribal Communities

Parivaar runs 874 in remote & poor tribal villages of 19 districts in Madhya Pradesh and 1 District each in Chhattisgarh and Jharkhand, serving more than 60,000 children with education and nutrition.
24*7 AMBULANCE SERVICES

INDIA'S LARGEST FREE 24*7 AMBULANCE SERVICES IN TRIBAL AREAS

Parivaar runs 94 free 24*7 Ambulance services in extremely remote and poor tribal areas of 23 districts of Madhya Pradesh. It has served more than 3 Lac cases, a significant number of which were life-saving.
MOBILE MEDICAL CLINICS
Parivaar runs 35 mobile clinics in extremely poor & remote tribal areas in 1827 villeges of 17 districts in Madhya Pradesh. In last 3 years, it has served 16 Lac cases.
VISION RESTORATION
Parivaar has held more than 5500 camps , serving more than 6 lakhs persons with 70 thousand surgeries in the last 3 years in remote tribal & other rural areas in MP.

Inspiration Behind Maromiya o Sampraday

The core inspiration behind Maromiya o Sampraday lies in the urgent need to preserve India’s vanishing tribal heritage and uplift the dignity of tribal women and children through cultural empowerment.

Founder’s Inspiration

Madhusree Hatial, a nationally recognized artist and award-winner (Sangeet Natak Akademi Yuva Puraskar 2018), has been deeply connected to the tribal heartlands of West Bengal, Jharkhand, and Odisha. Her close interaction with tribal artists, folk dancers, and women artisans made her realize: Tribal arts like Sohrai and Dokra—which are thousands of years old—are fading into oblivion, as the younger generation is unaware of their roots. Tribal women, the original keepers of this artistic heritage, face social neglect, economic instability, and cultural invisibility. There exists a vast reservoir of talent, tradition, and wisdom within these communities, which needs recognition, respect, and revival. Seeing this cultural erosion and social injustice firsthand, Madhusree was inspired to create a platform that would not just preserve art, but also restore pride, purpose, and prosperity to the tribal people.



A Vision Sparked by Heritage

What began as an individual’s passion has now grown into a movement—with over 5000 tribal beneficiaries, 60+ skill workshops, and thousands of trained women and children. The organization's work is fueled by the belief that:
“Culture is not just a memory of the past; it is a right to the future.”

Shri Vinayak Lohani receives the Padma Shri

We are extremely delighted to share that Shri Vinayak Lohani - Parivaar's Founder & President - has been chosen as one of the recipients of the Padma Shri awards by the Government of India for the year 2025.

To us, the award is a recognition of Vinayak ji's and Parivaar team's faith and conviction in the spirit of service, of the impact Parivaar has had and continues to have and of our donors and supporters who have supported Parivaar in this journey.

Thank you for being part of the journey with Parivaar. We are grateful for your support.

Parivaar is a humanitarian organization inspired by the spiritual and humanistic ideals of Sri Ramakrishna and Swami Vivekananda, with its chief institutions and projects based in West Bengal and Madhya Pradesh.
